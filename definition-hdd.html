HDD

  1. Home
  2. Definitions
  3. HDD
Description

  A storage device called an HDD (Hard Disk Drive) is used in computers and other electronic devices to store and retrieve digital data. It is made up of one or more spinning discs that can be magnetized or demagnetized to symbolize digital data.
  The read/write heads move across the surface of the disc in an HDD at high speeds to receive or write data are mounted on a spindle that rotates the discs at high speeds (See Figure 1).
  However, in comparison to other storage options like solid-state drives (SSDs), HDDs are also comparatively sluggish. This is because slower read and write speeds can occur as a consequence of the read/write heads having to physically move across the disk's surface to access data.
  Although SSDs are progressively replacing HDDs in many applications due to their faster performance and greater reliability.
  SSDs are either found inside a 3.5-inch bay or attached to a separate mount on the motherboard.
