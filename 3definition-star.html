Definition

  A star is a physical or logical arrangement of devices in a network. A central hub is connected to multiple devices creating a star like shape.
  The central hub is the center point of communication along with all the traffic that passes through it. Devices in a star talk to each other by sending data to the central hub first then to the final destination (desired device).
  A star layout helps with more control and management because the central hub can be eaily oversee the network. It is also simple to remove or add devices without affecting the whole network. However, if the central hub malfunctions, then the whole network is in trouble.
  Devices in a star can connect with each other wirelessly or with Ethernet cables.
